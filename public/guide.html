Overview
What this system does for you
After every care visit, the system automatically reaches out to clients to ask how things went. You don't need to do anything — it handles the messages, collects the responses, and brings everything back to your dashboard.
📱
Sends feedback requests automatically
As soon as you log a visit, a text message (or email) goes out to the client asking them to rate their experience. No manual follow-up needed.
⭐
Routes responses smartly
Happy clients (4–5★) are gently invited to share a Google review. Clients who had concerns (1–3★) are kept internal so your team can follow up privately.
📊
Shows you everything in one place
Your dashboard shows all responses, ratings, comments, and flags — so nothing falls through the cracks.
🔒
Private and respectful
Negative feedback never goes public. Clients' personal details are never shared. Every message includes an easy opt-out.
Step 1 — Clients Page
Adding a new client
Every client you serve should be in the system so feedback requests can go out after their visits.
1
Open the Clients page
Click Clients in the left-hand sidebar on your dashboard.
2
Click "+ Add Client"
You'll find this button in the top-right corner of the Clients page.
3
Fill in the client's details
Enter their full name (required), phone number, and email address.

Choose their preferred contact method — SMS or email. Most clients respond faster to text.
💡 You only need a name to save a client. You can add contact details later by clicking the client's row to edit.
4
Click "Add Client"
The client is saved and will appear in your client list, ready for visits to be logged.
Step 2 — Recording a Visit
How to log a visit
Logging a visit is what kicks off the whole feedback process. Once you record it, the system takes over — the client gets a message, and you'll see their response in the dashboard when they reply.
1
Find the client in the Clients list
Use the search bar to find them by name, phone, or email.
2
Click "+ Visit" on their row
You'll see a small button at the end of each client's row. You can also click the client's name to open their full profile, then choose "Record Visit" there.
3
Enter the visit date and caregiver's name
Both fields are required. The caregiver's name will be included in the feedback message so the client knows which visit you're asking about — "How was your visit with Maria Rodriguez on June 3rd?"
4
Click "Record Visit"
That's it. A feedback request is sent to the client automatically within minutes. You don't need to do anything else.
💡 If the client hasn't replied after 3 days, the system sends one gentle follow-up reminder on its own.
Step 3 — The Client Experience
What the client sees
The feedback form is designed to be as easy as possible — especially for older adults. No app, no login, no lengthy surveys.
1
Client receives a text or email
The message is personalised: "Hi Margaret, how was your visit with Maria Rodriguez on June 3rd? We'd love your feedback." — with a link to tap.
2
They tap one link — no login needed
The link opens a simple, mobile-friendly page. No passwords, no account creation. The whole form takes under 30 seconds.
3
They tap a star rating (1–5)
One tap selects their rating. They can optionally add a comment. That's all they have to do.
4
The system routes their response automatically
See the chart below for what happens next based on their rating.

Step 4 — Dashboard
Checking responses on your dashboard
Your dashboard is the home base for all feedback. Check it regularly — we recommend once a day, especially first thing in the morning.
📨
Total Requests
How many feedback requests have gone out in total, and how many responses have come back.
⭐
Average Rating
Your overall satisfaction score across all responses. Aim to keep this above 4.0.
📈
Response Rate
The percentage of clients who replied. A healthy response rate is above 40%.
🔔
Internal Flags
The number of low-rating responses waiting for a follow-up from your team. This should be kept at zero — action these promptly.
Reading the response table
TIP
Each row is one client's response
You'll see the client name, caregiver, visit date, star rating, their comment, and whether their response went to Google or was flagged internally.
TIP
Green "✓ Google" badge = review was prompted
This means the client was shown the Google review button after submitting their rating. They may or may not have followed through — check your Google Business profile to see if a review appeared.
TIP
Orange "🔔 Follow-up" badge = action needed
This client rated 1–3 stars. Their response was kept private. Your team needs to reach out to understand their concern and make things right.
Step 5 — Taking Action
How to respond to feedback
Every response in the table has an action button in the last column. What you do depends on whether the response was positive or a concern.
A
For 4–5★ responses — "Respond on Google"
Click the Respond on Google button. This opens your Google Business reviews page directly.

Find the client's review and click Reply to write a response. A short, warm reply goes a long way — it shows other families that Arise Cares is attentive and appreciative.
💡 Example reply: "Thank you so much, Margaret! We're thrilled to hear Maria made such a positive difference. It means everything to our team. 💛"
B
For 1–3★ responses — "🔔 Follow Up"
Click the Follow Up button. A note panel opens where you can:

• Write down what steps you're taking
• Assign it to the right team member (Care Manager, Supervisor, etc.)
• Record the outcome after you've spoken with the client
💡 Best practice: call the client within 24 hours. Acknowledge their concern, thank them for the feedback, and explain what action is being taken. Most clients respond very positively to a personal call.

Common Questions
Frequently asked questions
What if a client calls to complain before I see the feedback? ▼
Handle the call as you normally would. Afterwards, go to the dashboard, find their response in the table, click Follow Up, and log what happened during the call. This keeps everything in one place for the rest of your team.
Can a client accidentally see their rating or comment on the dashboard? ▼
No. The dashboard is for your team only. Clients only ever see their own personalised feedback form — they never see the dashboard or any other client's information.
What if a client submits feedback twice? ▼
Each feedback link is single-use. If a client tries to open their link a second time, they'll see a message letting them know their response was already received. No duplicate entries.
Do I need to log every visit, or only certain ones? ▼
Log every completed care visit. The more feedback you collect, the more useful your reporting becomes — and the better picture you have of each caregiver's performance and each client's satisfaction.
A client says they never received the text message. What do I do? ▼
First, check that their phone number is saved correctly in their client profile. If it is, they may have a carrier filter blocking automated texts — try switching their preferred contact to Email and logging a new visit. If neither works, let your system administrator know.
How do I know if a client actually left a Google review? ▼
The dashboard only tells you that a client was shown the Google review prompt — it can't confirm whether they actually posted a review. To check, log into your Google Business Profile and look at the Reviews section. New reviews from clients will appear there.
Can I see past feedback for a specific caregiver? ▼
Yes — on the dashboard's response table, each row shows the caregiver's name. You can see all feedback mentioning a specific caregiver by scrolling through the table. A more detailed filter view is coming in a future update.
What should I do if I see the same issue mentioned by multiple clients? ▼
That's a strong signal worth acting on. Log follow-up notes for each response, escalate to your supervisor, and consider addressing the pattern in your next team meeting. The Rating Distribution chart on the dashboard can help you spot trends over time.
